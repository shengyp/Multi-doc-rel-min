Coherence and Salience-Based Multi-Document Relationship Mining


Yongpan Sheng1 Zenglin Xu1 Gerard de Melo2

1University of Electronic Science and Technology of China, China   2Rutgers University, USA



1. Abstract

In today's interconnected world, there is an endless 24/7 stream of new articles appearing online. Faced with these overwhelming amounts of data, it is often helpful to consider only the key entities and concepts and their relationships. This is challenging, as relevant connections may be spread across a number of disparate articles and sources. In this paper, we propose a unified framework to aid users in quickly discerning salient connections and facts from a set of related documents, and presents the resulting information in a graph-based visualization. Specifically, given a set of relevant documents as input, first, we extract candidate facts from above sources by leveraging Open Information Extraction (Open IE) approaches. Then, we design a Two-Stage Candidate Triple Filtering (TCTF) approach to maintain only coherent facts associated with the specified document topic from the candidates and connect them in form of an initial graph. We also present a heuristic to obtain a final conceptual graph based on the graph in which the salient entities and concepts can be included. Our graph visualization then allows users to explore these connections. The experiments on two real-world data sets illustrate that our extraction approach achieves 3.2% higher on the average of F-score over several OpenIE baselines. We also further present an empirical evaluation of the quality of the final generated conceptual graph towards different topics on its coverage rate of topic entities and concepts, confidence score, and the compatibility of involved facts. Experimental results show the effectiveness of our proposed approach.
