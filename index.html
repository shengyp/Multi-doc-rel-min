A Framework for Graph-based Multi-Document Relation Exploration: A Case Study on the News Datasets


The 8th Nataional Conference on Social Media Processing（SMP 2019）


Yongpan Sheng1 Haojie Huang2

1University of Electronic Science and Technology of China, China   2University of New South Wales, Australia



1. Abstract

In today's interconnected world, there is an endless 24/7 stream of new articles appearing online. Faced with these overwhelming amounts of data, it is often helpful to consider only the key entities and concepts and their relationships. This is challenging, as relevant connections may be spread across a number of disparate articles and sources. In this paper, we propose a unified framework to aid users in quickly discerning salient connections and facts from a set of related documents and presents the resulting information in a graph-based visualization. Specifically, given a set of relevant documents as input, first, we extract candidate facts from the above sources by leveraging Open Information Extraction (Open IE) approaches. Then, we design a Two-Stage Candidate Triple Filtering (TCTF) approach to maintain only coherent facts associated with the specified document topic from the candidates and connect them in the form of an initial graph. We also present a heuristic to obtain a final conceptual graph based on the graph in which the salient entities and concepts can be included. Our graph visualization then allows users to explore these connections. The experiments on two real-world data sets illustrate that our extraction approach achieves 3.2% higher on the average of F-score over several OpenIE baselines. We also further present an empirical evaluation of the quality of the final generated conceptual graph towards different topics on its coverage rate of topic entities and concepts, confidence score, and the compatibility of involved facts. Experimental results show the effectiveness of our proposed approach.

2. Our Approach

Problem Formulation

Our task is aimed to assist users in quickly finding salient connections and facts from a collection of relevant articles. In summary, it can be best described as a combination of the following subtasks:

 

Data Preprocessing

NO. Tasks
1. Document Ranking
  1. Given a document topic $T$, select the words appearing in its relevant document collection $D = \{d_1, d_2, ..., d_M\}$ with sufficiently high frequency as topic words.
  2. Compute TF-IDF weights for each word.
  3. Rank top-$k$ documents in $D$ by TF-IDF weights, recorded as $D' = \{d_1, d_2, ..., d_k\}$.
2. Coreference Resolution
  • Resolve pronouns for each document $d_i \in D'$ using Stanford CoreNLP [cite]
3. Sentence Ranking
  • Compute the TextRank importance scores for all sentences in $d_i$
  • Consider only sentences with high score, denote by $S = \{s_1, s_2, ..., s_K\}$

Candidate Facts Extraction

Tasks
More descriptions here

Topic Coherence Estimation of Candidate Facts

Two-Stage Candidate Triple Filtering (TCTF)
Stage 1. Train a random forest classifier
  1. Randomly select a small fraction of triples $F_s \subset F_c$ under the document topic
  2. Divide $F_s$ into training set $F_{str}$, validation set $F_{sv}$ and test set $F_{st}$ with ratio 8:1:1
  3. Train a random forest classifier $M$ over $F_s$, obtain a F1-score $\theta$
Stage 2. Extend Coherent Triple Set
  1. Use the random forest $M$ to label each unlabelled triple $f \in F_c$; add $f$ into $F^*_l$ if it has high confidence
  2. Retrain model $M$ on $F^*_l$, obtain a new F1-score $\theta$
  3. Repeat until $\theta$ converges

Conceptual Graph Generation

No Tasks
1. Merge equivalent concepts and add relations
2. Computate node importance
3. Generate a conceptual graph
4 Visualize the conceptual graph

Conceptual graph construction.
